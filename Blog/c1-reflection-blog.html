Reflections

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?
I believe that the EDA experience is more than just learning to code, it's also about learning how to learn, and take care of yourself and understand yourself while doing so. I feel it's clear that you'll ultimately get out of the experience, what you put into it.

What are your impressions?
It appears that a lot of thought has gone into the process of learning, which is important to me as someone who will be investing a lot in the course. It's good to see the encouragement of a positive environment, as I feel that can be applied to different areas of our lives.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?
I feel there will be challenges with the level of group work, purely because I haven't had to manage this type of environment as consistently in my previous roles and studies. I'm also excited to see where I find I can excel, as well as learn about how I can improve myself, so I'm prepared to go into this with an open mind.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?
I don't think my expectations of EDA have changed, but more so the expectations of myself, and how I can make the most of the opportunity. I feel more motivated, and excited about my future.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous? I am excited, and nervous, but I also really enjoy challenges, and I am ready for this challenge. I'm looking forward to wrking with others who want to better themselves, and learn more about how I can be better myself, by working with those in my cohort.
