Daily Mindfulness

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'?
I learnt the importance of paying attention and the effect it can have on the speaker. When I was the speaker, it improved my confidence when speaking to know the listener was paying attention to what I had to say, even if I ran out of words to day on the topic. I somtimes find myself adding to conversations when someone else speaks if I have had a relevant experience, but I would like to learn to control this better, and just allow there to be silence in between, to give the other person sufficient time to speak.
Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?
I think all three, listening, looping and dipping will be usful in sustaining my practice. Looping in particular, as this would be a strong skill to acquire to ensure communication is kept to a high standard in many different envornments.
Any other musings?
I enjoyed the idea of making the medidation a challenge to yourself. For example, setting the bar low at first, and once this has been achieved, tackling a more complcated goal.
